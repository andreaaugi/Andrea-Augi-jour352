The Dark Side of Fast Fashion

The fashion industry is a major driver of environmental degradation.

By Andrea Augi

The demand from consumers for stylish yet reasonably priced apparel is driving the fast fashion industry's continued rapid expansion. Intense marketing tactics and the desire for rapid style changes are the main drivers of this growth, which drives ongoing increases in both production and consumption. The environment pays a price for this desire. Cheap clothing production involves unethical activities that are harmful to everyone involved. High carbon emissions, dangerous chemicals, and excessive water use are also part of the practices. Another significant and expanding issue is the disposal of clothing. Textiles and apparel are being dumped in landfills, which may take years to break down. These problems demonstrate the fashion industry's need to adopt more environmentally friendly practices.

The market for the fast fashion industry is expanding. The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 was the only major setback the industry had seen. The market dropped by 11.46% as a result of the pandemic. In the past ten years, the fashion industry has only gone through this one setback. In 2024, the global clothing market is expected to be worth $1.79 trillion. Approximately 92 million tons of textiles are thrown away globally each year. The average consumer in the United States alone discards over 81.5 pounds of clothing each year, contributing to an estimated 11.3 million tons of textile waste. The fast fashion industry will grow if consumers do not emphasize sustainable shopping practices. By choosing to shop sustainably, individuals can play a crucial role in protecting the environment before it’s too late.
